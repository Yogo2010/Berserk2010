Anime Berserk

**Berserk** (ベルセルク, Beruseruku) is a fantasy manga written by Kentaro Miura. The first volume was published in 1989, and the manga continues to be released to this day, maintaining its popularity. Additionally, several video games, a 25-episode anime adaptation, and three full-length animated films have been created based on the manga. As of now, 349 chapters of the manga have been published in Japan in the *Young Animal* magazine. The separate volume edition currently consists of 37 tankōbon volumes. In November 2009, the *Comics-Art* company announced that it had acquired the license for the Russian edition of the *Berserk* manga series, and on June 24, 2010, the first volume was released. One of the most popular adult manga series, with over 29 million copies sold, *Berserk* tells the life story of a mercenary named Guts. The events unfold in a world reminiscent of medieval Europe. The core of the plot revolves around mystical events surrounding Guts and his relationship with Griffith, the leader of the mercenary group known as the *Band of the Hawk* (鷹の団, *Taka no Dan*). Both the manga and the anime are characterized by extreme brutality, making the series a classic example of *dark fantasy*.

Heroes

Guts

Guts – The Black Swordsman (Berserk) Guts is the main protagonist of Berserk, a dark fantasy manga created by Kentaro Miura. Known as the Black Swordsman, he is a lone mercenary with a tragic past, wielding a massive sword known as the Dragon Slayer. Guts is characterized by his relentless determination, exceptional combat skills, and a deep-seated struggle between vengeance and his own humanity.

Griffith

Griffith – The White Hawk (Berserk) Griffith is one of the central characters in Berserk, the leader of the Band of the Hawk and the series’ main antagonist. He is charismatic, ambitious, and an exceptionally intelligent strategist who dreams of creating his own kingdom. His attractive appearance, refined manners, and strong will make him an ideal leader whom people are willing to follow even to their deaths.
